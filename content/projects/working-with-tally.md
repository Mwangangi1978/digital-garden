## Overview

[Tally](https://tally.xyz) is a foundational platform for building forms and managing decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) governance.

*Note: Tally recently announced they are [shutting down and pivoting to Cactus](https://newsletter.tally.xyz/p/tally-is-shutting-down)—a new smart contract and protocol deployment tool. You can read more about [the strategic turn to Cactus here](https://scopelift.co/blog/tally-is-now-cactus). This case study covers my core development work with the Tally team prior to this pivot.*

## The problem

In the decentralized governance space, user trust is paramount. Operating effectively requires highly reliable workflows where users have absolute clarity on system states and alerts. The team needed someone who could translate fuzzy product requirements into shipped, secure features that real DAOs could rely on.

## What I did

- **Notifications Manager:** Built and shipped the Tally Notifications Manager, handling complex state management and error handling to bring clarity to governance alerts.
- **End-to-End Integration:** Navigated the full stack, linking underlying data models to the front-end user interface while actively collaborating within the TallyTeam GitHub repositories.
- **Ecosystem Engagement:** Engaged directly with on-chain governance by analyzing and voting on various DAO proposals to better understand the user experience.
- **Prototyping & Analytics:** Utilized Tally's robust form infrastructure to build prototypes for my own projects (like "ThinkAgain"), actively configuring webhooks and hidden fields to capture structured data for attribution systems.

## Outcome

The Notifications Manager successfully streamlined communication workflows and enhanced user trust in the platform's governance operations. The engagement deepened my technical expertise in Web3 infrastructure and allowed me to build highly measurable, automated systems.

## Stack

Web3 stack, GitHub, Next.js/React, and Webhooks.
