**Sunday.**
It's not a day, it's a sigh. Lazy like Saturday. Cruel like Monday.

This Sunday, I woke up with no intention of catching any worm; at 10 a.m, and proceeded to have a lukewarm Pepsi and toast with red plum jam for breakfast.

What?

Don't look at me like that. Try it, then crucify me later.

Sundays, as the name suggests, have always been sunny -- especially in Nairobi.
It's the kind of sun that makes your T-shirt bite into your armpits and suckle your sweat.

Today was one of those days that invites you to sprawl on your dad's favorite couch and stare at the ceiling, thinking about the person you'd never text first but would reply to before they even hit send.

It made me wish there was an old man in the neighborhood who played the harmonica. Not to lull me into an afternoon siesta, but to transport me down memory lane.
To float me past the street signs of nostalgia and soft, blurry joy.

The world is a beautiful place.
The sound of kids laughing, louder on weekends.
The crisp blue sky.
And that neighbor whose cooking only comes alive the moment you decide to step outside for some air.

But my wonder was quickly vaporized by the crushing realization that tomorrow is Monday.

It's happened to all of us.
To the middle schooler whose Minecraft session is rudely interrupted by their mom asking if they've done their homework.
To the adult who gets an ill-timed Slack message while grilling with the boys.

And yet we never learn. We're always shocked.
As if the days of the week weren't tattooed into our brains in kindergarten.

**Sundays carry that quiet shadow.**
That gentle doom.

And so, I must surrender to my masochistic relationship with capitalism - my summer job.
The kind of grind that'll break your back by Friday... but make your mother proud.
The kind that enables you to care for the people you love.

It's the strange joy of work.
Of reaping the fruits of labor.
And the weekend, ironically, is part of the whole experience -- to give us time to hold weddings, hike forests, bury our dead, and breathe.

That's why capitalism *used to* respect the sanctity of the weekend.
That reverence is fading now.
Along with parts of our humanity.

But tomorrow, I'll go back to the job I enjoy; not because I adore sitting for 8 hours staring at a spreadsheet or a Google Doc, but because it keeps me yearning.
It keeps me coming back to *this*.
To you, dear reader.

And even when AI finally comes for my job, I'll still have someone to write to.
To caress their soul with words.

Until next weekend - when, together, we forget that Monday exists.
