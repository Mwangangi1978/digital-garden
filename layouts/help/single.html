02

Features

A few built-in tools to make reading more comfortable and exploration more useful.

Theme
Auto Light Dark
Switch between light, dark, or system-matched display. Your preference is saved across pages. The same toggles appear in the upper-right corner of every page.
Search
Search site
Full-text search across all notes, moments, watercolors, and pages. Type a word or phrase and results appear inline. Click any result to jump there, with your term highlighted on the page.
Graph
The knowledge graph shows every connection in the garden at once. Click a node to visit that page; hover to see its linked neighbours. Useful for discovering notes that aren't in the main archive.
03

How content works

Notes carry metadata that shows their state and how they connect to other pieces. Here's what each label means.

Status

complete
The piece is finished and unlikely to change significantly. Safe to cite or share.
draft
Still being written or revised. Ideas may shift; treat it as thinking-in-progress.
archived
Older work kept for continuity. May no longer reflect current thinking.

Connections

At the bottom of most notes you'll find a Connections section — explicit links to related pieces, each with a note on why they're connected. These same relationships are drawn in the graph.

Sidenotes

On longer notes, parenthetical thoughts appear as sidenotes — to the left of the main text on desktop, inline on mobile. They're optional reading; the main text is complete without them.

Backlinks

Below every note, a Backlinks section lists any other page on the site that links here. A reliable way to trace an idea backwards through the garden.
